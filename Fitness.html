Beginner

Push Ups

  • Starting Position: Begin in a plank position with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  • Lowering Phase: Lower your body until your chest nearly touches the ground, keeping your elbows close to your body.
  • Pushing Phase: Push yourself back up to the starting position by straightening your arms.
  • Breathing: Inhale as you lower your body, and exhale as you push back up.
  • Repetition: Repeat the movement for the desired number of repetitions, maintaining proper form throughout.
  • Variations: Try different variations as you get stronger, such as wide grip or narrow grip push-ups.
  • Rest: Allow adequate rest between sets to prevent fatigue.

Bodyweight Squats

  • Starting Position: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart.
  • Lowering Phase: Bend knees and push hips back as if sitting in a chair.
  • Pushing Phase: Straighten legs and return to starting position.
  • Breathing: Inhale down, exhale up.
  • Repetition: Perform 8-10 reps per set.
  • Rest: Allow adequate rest between sets.
  • Variations: Progress to jump squats or pistol squats for added challenge.

Crunches

  • Start: Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat.
  • Crunch: Engage core, lift upper body towards knees.
  • Lower: Slowly return upper body to floor.
  • Breathe: Exhale up, inhale down.
  • Reps: Aim for 10-15 per set.
  • Rest: Take breaks between sets.
  • Variations: Try bicycle or reverse crunches.

Exercises

Deadlift

  • Setup: Stand with feet hip-width apart, toes under the bar.
  • Grip: Grab the barbell with hands shoulder-width apart, palms facing down.
  • Lift: Keep back straight, push hips back, and lift the barbell by extending hips and knees.
  • Stand: Straighten up, keeping the barbell close to your body.
  • Lower: Bend at hips and knees to lower the barbell back to the floor.
  • Breathe: Inhale as you lower, exhale as you lift.
  • Reps: Perform 6-8 reps per set.
  • Rest: Take breaks between sets.
  • Variations: Progress to sumo or Romanian deadlifts for variety.

Dumbbell Shoulder Press

  • Setup: Sit on a bench with back support, holding dumbbells at shoulder height.
  • Press: Push dumbbells overhead until arms are fully extended.
  • Lower: Slowly lower dumbbells back to starting position.
  • Breathe: Exhale up, inhale down.
  • Reps: Aim for 8-12 reps per set.
  • Rest: Take breaks between sets.
  • Variations: Try alternating arms or using a barbell for variation.

Cable Rows

  • Setup: Attach a straight bar to a low pulley, sit on bench, feet on foot plates, and grab bar with overhand grip.
  • Pull: Pull bar towards lower chest while squeezing shoulder blades together.
  • Extend: Slowly return bar to starting position, fully extending arms.
  • Breathe: Exhale as you pull, inhale as you extend.
  • Reps: Aim for 10-12 reps per set.
  • Rest: Take breaks between sets.
  • Variations: Use different grip attachments for variety.

Cardio

Running

  • Start: Begin with a brisk walk to warm up.
  • Run: Gradually increase speed to a comfortable pace, focusing on landing softly with each step.
  • Duration: Aim for at least 20-30 minutes of continuous running.
  • Breathe: Maintain a steady rhythm of breathing, inhaling through the nose and exhaling through the mouth.
  • Cool Down: End your run with a slow jog or walk to gradually lower your heart rate.

Cycling

  • Setup: Adjust the seat and handlebars to a comfortable position.
  • Pedal: Start pedaling at a moderate pace, gradually increasing intensity as you warm up.
  • Intensity: Alternate between periods of high intensity and recovery, such as sprinting for 1 minute followed by 2 minutes of moderate cycling.
  • Duration: Aim for a total workout duration of 30-60 minutes.
  • Cool Down: Finish with a few minutes of easy pedaling to bring your heart rate back down.

Jump Rope

  • Setup: Hold the jump rope with handles in each hand, standing with feet hip-width apart.
  • Jump: Begin jumping over the rope, keeping knees slightly bent and landing softly on the balls of your feet.
  • Timing: Maintain a steady rhythm, jumping continuously for 1-2 minutes at a time.
  • Variations: Experiment with different footwork patterns, such as double unders or alternating feet.
  • Cooldown: Conclude with a few minutes of slow jumping or marching in place.
